# ❤️ Star-in-my-heart

> **"Don't just collect stars; let them illuminate your mind."** > Move your GitHub stars from the "digital graveyard" into your cognitive DNA.

---

## 🌟 The Mission: Why Star-in-my-heart?

Clicking the **Star** button takes 0.1 seconds. Truly understanding a complex repository can take 100 hours.

Most of our starred repos become **"shelfware"**—brilliant pieces of engineering that we admire but never actually absorb.

**Star-in-my-heart** is an AI-powered "Internalization Mentor." Its core philosophy is: **AI should not provide the answers; AI should design the path to the truth.** True knowledge only sticks when you write the code, draw the diagrams, and explain the logic yourself.

---

## 🧠 Core Philosophy: Socratic Internalization

Unlike traditional AI summary tools, this project follows three strict principles:

1. **No Spoon-feeding**: AI is forbidden from giving long-winded explanations or summaries.
2. **Output-First**: You must leave "breadcrumbs of thought" (code comments, lab notes, refactoring drafts) in your local repo before the AI unlocks the next level of insight.
3. **Destructive Learning**: AI encourages you to perform "micro-surgeries" on the code—breaking things to understand how they work.

---

## 🛠️ Key Features

### 1. The Archeologist (Deep Context Scanning)

The AI works behind the scenes to prepare your mental map:

* **Lineage Tracing**: Which paper inspired this? Who are its ancestors?
* **Ecosystem Positioning**: What specific pain point does this solve? Who are its rivals?
* **The 20/80 Rule**: Automatically identifies the entry points and the 20% of core logic that powers 80% of the functionality.

### 2. The Icebreaker (Critical Inquiry)

When you open a starred repo, the AI challenges you with three "cannot-be-Googled" questions:

* *"Without looking at the docs, where is the state managed? Why that specific pattern?"*
* *"If this project had to scale 10x tomorrow, which line of code would break first?"*
* *"What is the most 'clever' or 'controversial' variable naming you see here?"*

### 3. The Sandbox (Guided Experimentation)

AI guides you through "live drills":

* **Stress Testing**: Instructions on modifying a parameter to observe memory/latency spikes.
* **Logic Swap**: A challenge to replace an existing design pattern with another and document the trade-offs.

### 4. The Feynman Checkpoint (The Ultimate Test)

When you think you've "learned it," the AI roleplays as a curious beginner. Only when you can explain the complex logic in plain English does the Star truly move into your **Heart**.
